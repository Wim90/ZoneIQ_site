ZoneIQ

A powerful macOS app that provides deep insights into your Orangetheory Fitness® workout data. Analyze your performance, track trends, and make data-driven decisions about your training.

Key Features

📊 Performance Dashboard

Comprehensive overview of your workout metrics including calories, splat points, heart rate zones, and consistency metrics. View your data across customizable date ranges.

📈 Period Comparison

Compare your performance across different time periods to see if you're training smarter, not just harder. Identify trends and patterns in your workout data.

🎯 Splat Zone Analysis

Track your time in the optimal 12-20 splat zone and identify overtraining risks. Get insights on whether you're consistently hitting your target zones.

🏃 Equipment Performance

Detailed metrics for treadmill and rower performance including distance, pace, watts, and cadence. Understand how you perform on different equipment.

📅 Long-term Trends

Visualize your performance evolution over weeks, months, and quarters. See if you're improving, plateauing, or need to adjust your training approach.

🤖 AI-Powered Insights

On supported devices, Apple Intelligence generates personalized insights and recommendations based on your workout history. All processing happens on-device for privacy.

🔒 Privacy First

All data processing happens locally on your Mac. Your workout data never leaves your device. Credentials are stored securely using macOS Keychain.

💻 macOS Native

Built specifically for macOS with a native interface optimized for larger screens. Perfect for deep analysis sessions with side-by-side comparisons and detailed charts.

What You Can Analyze

  • Training consistency: Are you showing up regularly and staying in the optimal zones?
  • Performance patterns: Do you perform better at certain times, with specific coaches, or in particular class formats?
  • Progress tracking: How has your performance changed over time? Are you improving or plateauing?
  • Overtraining detection: Identify streaks of high-intensity days that might indicate overtraining risk.
  • Class format optimization: Compare your performance across 2G, 3G, Tread 50, and other class types to optimize your booking strategy.

How It Works

ZoneIQ is an independent application and is not affiliated with Orangetheory Fitness®. You sign in with your own Orangetheory Fitness® account—the same credentials you use for the official app. ZoneIQ can access only your workout history, and only after you authenticate. Your data is not public, is not shared with other users, and your login is never sent to the developer. Workout information is downloaded and processed entirely on your Mac, so you stay in control while getting deeper analytics.

Note: This app is not an official Orangetheory Fitness® product. It is provided as-is by an independent developer and depends on your ability to sign in to your Orangetheory Fitness® account.

Information the Application Needs (No Collection by the Developer)

The developer of ZoneIQ does not collect, receive, or have access to your personal data. You are not sending your data to the developer.

To provide the service (workout stats, insights, and comparisons), the application on your Mac needs to read certain data from your Orangetheory Fitness® account. That access happens only on your device: the app fetches data so it can show it to you and compute metrics locally. Nothing is sent to the developer or to any servers operated by the developer.

Data the application uses on your device:

  • Health and Fitness Data: Workout dates, calories burned, splat points, heart rate zone data, workout duration
  • Equipment Performance Data: Treadmill metrics (distance, pace, incline), rower metrics (distance, watts, split times, cadence)
  • Account Information: Your name and home studio location (for display only)
  • Authentication Credentials: Your Orangetheory Fitness® login (only if you choose to save them; stored in macOS Keychain on your Mac)

Summary: The app needs this data to function. The developer does not collect it.

Disclaimer: ZoneIQ is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Orangetheory Fitness®. It is an independent application.

Data Storage and Security

All data processing occurs locally on your Mac. Your workout information is stored only on your device:

  • Credentials: If you choose to save your login credentials, they are stored in the macOS Keychain on your Mac (industry-standard encryption)
  • Workout Data: Cached workout data is stored locally on your Mac for faster access. You can clear it at any time via "Clear Cache" in the app
  • No data is sent to the developer or to any servers operated by the developer

The application uses the security measures provided by macOS (e.g. Keychain, local storage) to protect data on your device.

How the Application Uses Your Data

The application uses your workout data only on your device to:

  • Display performance metrics and statistics
  • Generate insights and comparisons between time periods
  • Provide recommendations and trend analysis
  • Enable period-to-period performance comparisons

All of this is done locally on your Mac. The developer does not use your data. Your data is never used for advertising, sale to third parties, sharing with others, or any purpose other than the app's core functionality.

Apple Intelligence

On supported devices (Apple Silicon Macs running macOS 15.0 or later), ZoneIQ uses Apple Intelligence to generate AI-powered insights about your workout performance.

When Apple Intelligence is available:

  • Your workout data (calories, splat points, consistency metrics, and equipment performance) is processed locally using on-device AI models
  • No workout data is sent to Apple or any external servers
  • All processing occurs entirely on your device
  • Apple Intelligence generates personalized insights, recommendations, and trend analysis based on your workout history

Apple Intelligence processing is automatic when your hardware supports it. You can identify Apple Intelligence insights in the app by the "Apple Intelligence Insights" section in the Dashboard.

For more information about Apple Intelligence privacy, please refer to Apple's Privacy Policy.

Data Sharing and Third-Party Services

The developer does not sell, rent, or share your data with anyone. The developer does not receive your data.

The application does not integrate with third-party analytics, advertising, data collection, social media, or data brokers.

The app communicates only with Orangetheory Fitness® servers to retrieve your workout data (e.g. via the same type of authentication as the official OTF app). That is required for the app to work. Your data is not shared with any other third parties, including Apple (except as described in the Apple Intelligence section).

Your Privacy Rights

You have full control over your data and the following rights:

  • Right to Access: You can view all your data within the app at any time
  • Right to Delete: You can delete your saved credentials at any time by unchecking "Remember credentials" when logging in, or by signing out
  • Right to Data Deletion: You can clear all cached workout data by using the "Clear Cache" option in the Data menu (menu bar). This removes all locally stored workout data and equipment details from your device
  • Right to Stop Data Collection: You can stop using the app at any time, which will prevent further data access
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You can revoke access to your Orangetheory Fitness® account at any time by signing out or uninstalling the app

Cached data refers to workout information that has been downloaded from your Orangetheory Fitness® account and stored locally on your Mac for faster access. This includes workout dates, calories, splat points, heart rate data, and equipment metrics (treadmill and rower details). Clearing this cache will remove all stored workout data, but you can re-download it by refreshing your data.

Data Retention

Data is kept only on your device and only as long as needed for the app to work:

  • Cached Workout Data: On your Mac until you clear the cache or uninstall the app
  • Credentials: In macOS Keychain until you delete them or sign out
  • The developer does not operate any servers, so there is no server-side retention of your data

When you clear cached data or uninstall the app, that data is removed from your device.
